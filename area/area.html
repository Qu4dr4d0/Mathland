Circle

It is a figure formed by a flat surface that is limited by a circumference ("curved line"). It is considered non-polygonal because it does not have a straight line.

Triangle

Trilateral flat geometric shape, that is, formed by three sides and three angles, which together add up to 180º

Square

Flat geometric shape that features four equal sides and angles. It is considered a polygon, called a quadrilateral, because it has 4 straight segments.

Rectangle

It is a flat geometric figure formed by four sides, two of which are smaller than the others, thus differentiating it from squares.

Pentagon

A pentagon has 5 sides, the top two being equal and the two sides and base being equal.

Hexagon

All sides of a hexagon are the same length and all interior angles are 120 degrees.

Trapeze

A trapeze is a short horizontal bar hung by ropes or metal straps from a ceiling support. It is an aerial apparatus commonly found in circus performances.

Parallelogram

a parallelogram is a simple (non-self-intersecting) quadrilateral with two pairs of parallel sides.

Ellipsis

Similar to the circle, the oval figure also has a rounded shape, but is flatter. It is considered non-polygonal because it does not have a straight line.

Circle crown

We call a circular crown to the part composed by two circumferences that possess the same centre.

Circle sector

A circular sector, also known as circle sector or disk sector, is the portion of a disk enclosed by two radii and an arc.
